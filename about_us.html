The frustration


One of the most frustrating parts of FIRST LEGO League for us was programming the robot. It was time consuming as each turn and motion had to be programmed, tested, tweaked and so on. One 3Geared member made it his personal mission to devise a faster and simpler way to program our robot, and this ambition grew into FLS.

Summer of 2019. was a fruitful one for 6 young programmers with a dream. They dreamed of solving a problem of painful LEGO robot programming. The idea was to build a web app where they can describe the movements of the robot faster than doing it through official LEGO software.

Many obstacles were on their path, but these young minds already had a good foundation in mathematics, physics, robotics and programming - “obstacles will be defeated“ they all agreed.

On their adventure they seeked guidance of experienced Software and Hardware Engineers, Josip, Fran and Vlatko. They helped the young team to master ancient languages of HTML, CSS, and the dreaded JavaScript.

Testing, design tweaking and wrapping up a stable app happened after the summer and at the start of the new FLL season.

Our solution


We believe that sharing new techniques and ideas is a key element of all competitions because it challenges us to always keep improving and innovating.

Our mission with FLS is to make life easier for fellow EV3 enthusiast, and also inspire others to challenge themselves to improve upon the existing and create original solutions to problems.

FLS is a free tool that can be used by anyone to quickly, easily and intuitively program complex robot movements.

FLS consists of 2 parts: the EV3 MyBlock and the FLS website. The FLS website is where the “brains” are, it calculates the needed angles, distances, and speeds and outputs them to a downloadable text file.

The EV3 MyBlock has only 1 input, which is the name of the text file you download. This file is then “read” by the EV3 MyBlock which makes the robot move.
